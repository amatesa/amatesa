# 01 — Portfolio Narrative

## Purpose

This document defines how Alejandro's story should be told.

It does not define visual design.

It does not define implementation.

It defines the emotional and intellectual journey that visitors should experience while exploring the portfolio.

Every section, animation, interaction and piece of copy should reinforce this narrative.

---

# The Reader

The portfolio is written for people.

Not algorithms.

Not ATS systems.

Not search engines.

The primary audience is someone trying to answer one question:

"Would I want this person working on my team?"

The portfolio should answer that question naturally.

Never explicitly.

---

# What We Want the Visitor to Feel

Curiosity.

Confidence.

Respect.

By the end of the experience, visitors should feel they have discovered a person rather than consumed a résumé.

---

# What We Don't Want

We do not want admiration.

We do not want surprise.

We do not want hype.

We want trust.

---

# Narrative Arc

The story unfolds in six stages.

Understand.

↓

Think.

↓

Build.

↓

Learn.

↓

Explore.

↓

Connect.

Every section should naturally encourage the visitor to continue.

No section should feel isolated.

---

# The Central Question

The portfolio should quietly answer one question.

What happens when Alejandro encounters a problem he has never seen before?

Every project.

Every experience.

Every paragraph.

Every interaction.

Should reinforce the same answer.

He understands it.

He learns what is necessary.

He builds a solution.

He improves it.

---

# Tone

Calm.

Thoughtful.

Honest.

Curious.

Never loud.

Never arrogant.

Never overly humble.

Never defensive.

Never corporate.

Never generic.

---

# Writing Style

Write like someone explaining how they think.

Not like someone applying for a job.

Avoid slogans.

Avoid motivational language.

Avoid exaggerated confidence.

Prefer observations over declarations.

---

# Evidence Before Claims

Never say:

"I am analytical."

Instead show analysis.

Never say:

"I am adaptable."

Instead tell a story where adaptation happened.

Never say:

"I am curious."

Instead reveal curiosity through questions, experiments and decisions.

---

# Reading Rhythm

Alternate between:

Short sentences.

↓

Longer reflections.

↓

Visual evidence.

↓

Interaction.

↓

Silence.

The reader should never encounter large uninterrupted walls of text.

Whitespace is part of the narrative.

---

# Cognitive Load

Each section should communicate one idea.

Not five.

If a paragraph introduces a second concept, split it.

Reduce friction.

Reward curiosity.

---

# Progression

The visitor should slowly realize something.

At first:

"This person has interesting experience."

Later:

"This person thinks differently."

Finally:

"I can imagine working with this person."

That realization should emerge naturally.

Never be stated directly.

---

# Authenticity

Imperfections are allowed.

Uncertainty is allowed.

Learning is allowed.

Exaggeration is not.

If a project is academic, say so.

If a technology is new, say so.

Honesty creates credibility.

---

# Relationship with Technology

Technology appears as evidence.

Not identity.

The portfolio never communicates:

"I know Tool X."

It communicates:

"I learned Tool X because the problem required it."

The order matters.

---

# Relationship with Projects

Projects are not trophies.

Projects are evidence of thinking.

Every project should answer:

Why was it built?

What problem existed?

What decisions were made?

What changed afterwards?

---

# Relationship with Failure

Not every experiment succeeds.

That is acceptable.

What matters is the quality of the reasoning.

Growth is more valuable than perfection.

---

# Recognition

Recognition is presented as the consequence of meaningful work.

Never as the objective.

The portfolio should communicate pride without vanity.

Confidence without ego.

---

# Visual Narrative

Movement supports understanding.

Interaction rewards exploration.

Animation guides attention.

Nothing moves simply because it can.

Every interaction should feel intentional.

---

# Emotional Goal

The visitor should leave with one lasting impression.

Alejandro is someone who improves the things he works on.

Not because he has to.

Because that is how he naturally thinks.

---

# Final Thought

The portfolio should never feel finished.

It should feel alive.

Like the work of someone who is still learning, still building and still improving.

That is not a weakness.

It is the most honest representation of who Alejandro is.
