# 01 — Vision

## Project

Personal Portfolio Website

Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This portfolio is not intended to function as an online résumé.

Its purpose is to communicate how I think, how I approach problems, and how I build digital products.

The visitor should leave with a clear understanding of my way of working before reading my work history or technical skills.

The portfolio should feel like entering a creative workspace where ideas become products through curiosity, analysis, experimentation and continuous improvement.

---

# Core Statement

> I understand problems before building solutions.

Everything inside the portfolio should reinforce this idea.

Projects, animations, navigation, typography, illustrations and interactions exist to support this message.

Nothing should exist only because it looks impressive.

---

# Portfolio Philosophy

The portfolio is built around a simple belief:

Understanding the problem is more valuable than immediately choosing a technology.

Technologies evolve.

Ways of thinking remain.

The portfolio represents a professional who learns continuously, adapts quickly and enjoys building better systems.

---

# Visitor Experience

The portfolio should feel like exploration rather than navigation.

The visitor should never feel they are reading a résumé.

Instead, they should feel that every section reveals another part of the workspace.

Each section answers one question.

Who is Alejandro?

↓

How does he think?

↓

What has he built?

↓

What is he learning?

↓

How can we work together?

The experience should encourage curiosity.

---

# Emotional Goals

The portfolio should communicate:

• clarity

• confidence

• curiosity

• craftsmanship

• continuous improvement

• creativity through structure

It should never communicate:

• arrogance

• complexity for the sake of complexity

• visual noise

• unnecessary animations

• "look how many technologies I know"

---

# Design Principles

Every design decision should satisfy these principles.

## 1.

Content before decoration.

## 2.

Motion with purpose.

Animations explain transitions.

They never distract.

## 3.

Minimalism without emptiness.

Whitespace creates focus.

Not boredom.

## 4.

Creativity through interaction.

Originality comes from behavior.

Not from excessive visual effects.

## 5.

Consistency.

Every page should feel like part of the same product.

---

# Visual Metaphor

The portfolio represents a digital workbench.

A place where ideas are explored, prototypes are created and systems are improved.

Not an office.

Not a laboratory.

Not a game.

A workspace.

---

# Narrative

The portfolio tells one story.

Understand.

↓

Analyze.

↓

Build.

↓

Learn.

↓

Improve.

↓

Repeat.

Everything else supports this narrative.

---

# What the Visitor Should Remember

If someone spends only one minute inside the portfolio, they should leave thinking:

"This person understands problems before building solutions."

and

"I would trust this person to work on complex digital products."

---

# Success Criteria

The portfolio succeeds if visitors:

• understand who Alejandro is within the first 15 seconds

• remember the way he thinks instead of a list of technologies

• explore more than one project

• visit GitHub

• download the résumé

• contact Alejandro

---

# Non Goals

The portfolio is NOT intended to:

be a visual experiment

imitate Awwwards

show every technology

show every project

maximize animations

replace LinkedIn

replace GitHub

Everything that does not support the purpose of the portfolio should be removed.

---

# Guiding Question

Every future decision should answer one question:

Does this help visitors understand how Alejandro thinks?

If the answer is no,

it does not belong in the portfolio.
